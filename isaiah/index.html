The Maker

Isaiah has been making things since early 2024 — starting with stained glass panels cut and soldered by hand, then expanding to wood carving, forge work, and blademaking. In the fall of 2025 he poured concrete and built his own backyard forge from scratch, and the ambition of his work has grown steadily ever since: from pocket knives to a kukri to a full-length long sword, and most recently, hand-forged damascus steel. Each piece is made with patience, care, and a healthy appreciation for sharp things.

The Crafts

Stained Glass

Cutting, grinding, and soldering coloured glass into panels and decorative pieces. Isaiah started with a simple flower in 2024 and worked up to a figurative scene of sisters at the beach, with a hand-built wooden frame.

View photos

Wood Carving

Gouges, chisels, and knives turning raw wood into useful and decorative objects — from a picture frame and a carved obelisk to a spoon, a burl bowl, and a delicate jewelry tree.

View photos

Blacksmithing

Iron and steel heated to glowing and shaped by hammer and anvil. Early experiments with forged rings led to building a full backyard forge in 2025 — then the real work began.

View photos

Knifemaking

Grinding steel to a blade, fitting wooden scales, pinning the handle, and finishing an edge sharp enough to shave with. Isaiah has made a growing collection of knives including a bowie and a curved kukri.

View photos

Swordsmithing

His most ambitious project: a full-length long sword with a forged curved crossguard, wood grip, and polished double-edged blade — completed in January 2026 and unveiled by firelight.

View photos

Damascus Steel

Forge-welding alternating layers of steel together, then etching the blade to reveal a flowing pattern within. Isaiah tackled this ancient technique in 2026 — the result speaks for itself.
